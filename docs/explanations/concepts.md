# 📖 AI in Plain English
### Understanding the Concepts without the Jargon.

WikiArchitect uses some advanced technology under the hood. Here is what those terms mean in everyday language.

---

### 🏛️ The Vault
**The Concept:** Your digital brain.
**The Detail:** This is the folder on your computer where everything is stored. It’s private, searchable, and belongs entirely to you.

### 📚 The Librarian (LLM)
**The Concept:** Your personal researcher.
**The Detail:** The "Large Language Model" (like Gemma 4) is the AI brain that power-reads your files. Think of it as a highly trained librarian who has read every single page in your vault and can answer questions about them.

### 📐 The Architect Review (Diff)
**The Concept:** The final "Yes" or "No."
**The Detail:** Before the Librarian writes anything into your notes, it shows you a "Difference" (Diff). This highlights exactly what it wants to add or change, so you can review and approve it. You are the Architect; the AI is the builder.

### 🧠 Semantic Search (RAG)
**The Concept:** Search by *meaning*, not just words.
**The Detail:** Instead of just looking for exact words, WikiArchitect looks for *ideas*. If you search for "Health," it might find notes about "Nutrition" or "Exercise" because it understands the connection.

### 🗺️ Map of Content (MOC)
**The Concept:** A table of contents that writes itself.
**The Detail:** An MOC is a special page that gathers links to related notes. The Librarian generates these to help you navigate large clusters of information.

### 🧱 Vault Boundary
**The Concept:** The digital security fence.
**The Detail:** WikiArchitect is programmed with a strict rule: *Never look outside the vault.* This ensures that even though the AI is powerful, it can’t peek at your private system files or personal documents outside the project folder.
