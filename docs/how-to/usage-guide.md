# 📚 WikiArchitect: The Architect's Manual
### Master Your Knowledge. Augment Your Thinking.

WikiArchitect isn't just a place to store files—it's a system for **building connections**. This guide will walk you through the professional practices of "Knowledge Architecture."

---

## 🏛️ The Philosophy: Human-in-the-Loop
WikiArchitect follows a simple rule: **The Librarian proposes, the Architect decides.**
The AI (The Librarian) is incredibly fast at reading and linking, but you (The Architect) have the context and the wisdom. By reviewing every change, you ensure your vault remains a source of high-quality, verified truth.

---

## 🛠️ The Core Workflow: Ingest → Review → Chat

### 1. Adding Knowledge (Ingesting)
When you add a file (PDF or Text), the Librarian performs a **Deep Scan**.
- It extracts the core concepts.
- It looks for connections to your existing notes.
- It proposes new pages if it sees a "gap" in your knowledge.

### 2. The Decision (Review Dialog)
This is your primary workstation.
- **Why it matters**: AI can sometimes be too creative ("hallucinations"). This screen lets you catch those moments.
- **The side-by-side view**: See exactly what is being added. If you like the connections, hit **Apply**. If not, discard them.

### 3. Talking to Your Vault
On the right side of the screen is the **Assistant Panel**.
- **Ask Anything**: "What did I learn about AI safety last month?"
- **Context Awareness**: The Librarian only answers using your files. It won't make up facts from the internet.
- **Automatic Research**: If you ask about something you haven't written yet, the Librarian will tell you it's missing!

---

## 🗺️ Visualizing Thought: The Knowledge Graph
Click the **Graph** tab to see your digital brain come to life.
- **Blue Bubbles**: Your notes and files.
- **Orange Bubbles**: Broad concepts or categories.
- **The Lines**: Relationships identified by you or the Librarian.
- **Discovery**: Use the graph to find "islands" of information that aren't linked to anything yet.

---

## ⚡ Pro Habits for Power Users

### A. The "Wiki-Link"
Whenever you're writing, use double brackets like this: `[[Important Concept]]`. This tells the Librarian that you want to create a permanent connection to that idea.

### B. Use the Librarian Menu
In the top menu, you can ask the Librarian to perform maintenance:
- **Update the Map**: Refresh your `MOC.md` to show your latest discoveries.
- **Generate Briefing**: Get a summary of everything you've done in the last 7 days.

### C. Privacy Check
Remember: **Your data never leaves your computer.** WikiArchitect is fully functional even without an internet connection once your local models are downloaded.

---

## ❓ Common Questions

**Q: Why is it taking a while to read my PDF?**
A: Reading and "thinking" about a document takes power! Your computer is doing a lot of heavy lifting to understand the context. Large files (50+ pages) may take a minute or two.

**Q: I don't see any links in the graph.**
A: Use the **Assistant** to ask: *"Can you suggest some connections between my recent notes?"* The Librarian will analyze them and propose links for you to approve.
