# 🚀 Getting Started with WikiArchitect
### Your First AI Insight in 3 Steps.

Welcome! WikiArchitect is designed to be simple. You don't need to be an AI expert to build a world-class knowledge base. Follow this guide to see the magic happen for the first time.

---

## Step 1: Add Your First Source 📄
Find a PDF or text file you've been meaning to summarize or connect to other ideas.
1.  Open WikiArchitect.
2.  Click the **Ingest (+)** button in the top toolbar.
3.  Choose your file.
4.  Wait a moment while the **Librarian** reads and understands it.

## Step 2: Shake Hands with the Librarian 🤝
WikiArchitect won't change your notes without asking. After the Librarian reads your file, the **Architect Review** window will pop up.
1.  You'll see a "Proposed Change" on the right side.
2.  Look at the **connections** the Librarian found (e.g., links like `[[Quantum Physics]]`).
3.  Click **Apply** to save these changes to your wiki.
4.  Check the **Log** in the bottom panel—you've just added your first piece of permanent knowledge!

## Step 3: Ask a Question 💬
Now that your file is part of the "Vault," you can talk to it.
1.  Go to the **Assistant Panel** on the right.
2.  Type a question like: *"What are the three main takeaways from the file I just added?"*
3.  The Librarian will answer using *only* your data, citing precisely where it found the information.

---

### 💡 Pro Tip: The Knowledge Graph
Click the **Graph** tab in the main view. You'll see your new note as a blue bubble. As you add more files, you'll see lines form between them—these are the "Smart Connections" being built by your AI Librarian!
